How to dress for

Rectangle

Tips
  • Define the waist

    • - Pull in the waist with belts or nipped garments.

    - Wear darker colours around your waist area such as a dark belt.

    - Don't tuck tops into trousers or skirts.

    Tops

    choose a neckline such as a wide v-neck, boatneck, sweetheart or square neck to make your shoulders look wider.

    wide v-neck
    boat neck
    sweetheart neck
    square neck
    Skirts

    a-line skirts with high waists are the perfect choice for you. Choose a pencil or layered skirts are an ideal way to add volume to the lower body. Bright and light colours also help create volume.

    A-line skirts
    pencil skirts
    full skirts
    layered skirts
    Trousers

    choosing trousers with hip, thigh, leg details or high-waisted such as cargo, bootcut, wide-leg or turn-up trousers to create even further interest on your lower body.

    cargo trousers
    bootcut trousers
    wide-leg trousers
    turn-up trousers
    Dress

    A great dress for a rectangle body is balanced on top and bottom whilst pulling inthe waist. You can add colour-blocking around the waist or add a dark belt to a dress.

    wrap dress
    empire dress
    princess-seam dress
    shift dress
